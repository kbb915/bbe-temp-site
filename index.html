A Different Point of Intervention

More process will not repair leadership avoidance.

What's Often Added
  • More meetings and executive oversight
  • More reporting and performance tracking
  • New systems, structures, or reorganizations
  • Escalation that further centralizes decisions
What's Often Missing
  • Leaders enforcing standards
  • Leaders exercising and distributing authority
  • Leaders modeling and sustaining accountability
  • Leaders addressing the behaviors that determine organizational movement
Adding systems, people, or new processes without first addressing the underlying behaviors is like replacing the engine while the parking brake is still engaged. More capacity. Same constraint.
